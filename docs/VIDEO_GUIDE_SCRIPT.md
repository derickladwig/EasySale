# EasySale POS System - Video Guide Script

## Video Information
- **Total Runtime**: ~15-18 minutes
- **Format**: Screen recording with voiceover
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 (Full HD)
- **Target Audience**: New users, store staff training

---

## Chapter 1: Introduction (0:00 - 1:30)

### Narration
> "Welcome to EasySale, a modern point-of-sale system designed for retail businesses of all sizes. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to get started with EasySale.
>
> EasySale is a white-label, multi-tenant system that works offline-first, meaning you can continue processing sales even without an internet connection. Your data syncs automatically when connectivity returns.
>
> Let's begin by logging in and exploring the interface."

### Actions
1. Show the EasySale logo/splash screen
2. Display the login page
3. Highlight key features on screen:
   - Offline-first operation
   - Multi-store support
   - Touch-optimized interface

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Animated logo intro
- [ ] Feature bullet points overlay
- [ ] Transition to login screen

## Chapter 2: Login & First Look (1:30 - 3:30)

### Narration
> "Here's the login page. Notice the modern glass-morphism design with the status panel on the right showing your database connection and sync status.
>
> You can log in using either a password or a PIN for quick access. Let me show you both methods.
>
> For this demo, we'll log in with the admin account we created during setup.
>
> Notice the 'Remember username' checkbox - this saves your username for faster login next time.
>
> After logging in, you'll see the main dashboard. Let's explore the navigation."

### Actions
1. Point to the status panel (Database: Connected, Sync: Online)
2. Show the Password/PIN toggle tabs
3. Enter your admin credentials (created during setup)
4. Check "Remember username"
5. Click "Sign In"
6. Wait for dashboard to load

### Expected Results
- Login page displays correctly
- Status panel shows "Connected" and "Online"
- Successful login redirects to Dashboard

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Highlight status panel with callout
- [ ] Circle the auth method tabs
- [ ] Arrow pointing to Remember checkbox
- [ ] Success animation on login

## Chapter 3: Dashboard Overview (3:30 - 5:30)

### Narration
> "Welcome to the Dashboard - your command center for daily operations.
>
> At the top, you'll see today's key statistics: total sales, transaction count, average transaction value, and items sold. These update in real-time as you process sales.
>
> Below that are Quick Actions - large, touch-friendly buttons for the most common tasks. Tap 'New Sale' to start a transaction, 'Product Lookup' to search inventory, and so on.
>
> On the left side is the main navigation sidebar. On mobile devices, this becomes a bottom navigation bar.
>
> The Alerts panel shows important notifications like low stock warnings. The Recent Transactions panel shows your latest sales activity.
>
> Notice the sync status indicator in the header - it shows 'Online' with a green icon when connected."

### Actions
1. Point to each statistics card
2. Hover over Quick Action buttons
3. Click through sidebar navigation items (don't navigate yet)
4. Scroll to show Alerts and Recent Transactions
5. Point to sync status in header

### Expected Results
- Dashboard loads with statistics
- Quick actions are visible and clickable
- Navigation sidebar is functional

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Highlight each stat card sequentially
- [ ] Callout boxes for Quick Actions

## Chapter 4: Point of Sale - Making a Sale (5:30 - 9:00)

### Narration
> "Let's process a sale. Click 'Sell' in the navigation or the 'New Sale' quick action.
>
> The Sell page is divided into two panels. On the left is your product catalog with search and category filters. On the right is the shopping cart.
>
> Let's search for a product. Type in the search bar or use a barcode scanner - the system accepts scanner input automatically.
>
> You can filter by category using these tabs. Notice each category has an icon for quick identification.
>
> Click a product to add it to the cart. The quantity defaults to 1 - use the plus and minus buttons to adjust.
>
> At the top of the cart, you can select a customer. This is optional for walk-in sales but useful for tracking customer purchases and loyalty.
>
> Below the cart items, you'll see quick actions for discounts and coupons. The 'Clear' button empties the entire cart.
>
> At the bottom is the checkout section showing subtotal, tax, and total. Choose your payment method - Cash, Card, or Other - to complete the sale."

### Actions
1. Navigate to Sell page
2. Type "product" in search bar
3. Click a category tab
4. Toggle between Grid and List view
5. Click a product to add to cart
6. Adjust quantity using +/- buttons
7. Add a second product
8. Click "Select Customer" (show the modal)
9. Point to Discount and Coupon buttons
10. Show the totals calculation
11. Click "Cash" payment button

### Expected Results
- Products display in catalog
- Items add to cart correctly
- Totals calculate automatically
- Payment buttons are active when cart has items

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Search bar focus highlight
- [ ] Category tab selection animation
- [ ] Cart item add animation
- [ ] Quantity change highlight
- [ ] Total calculation callout

## Chapter 5: Product Lookup (9:00 - 11:00)

### Narration
> "The Lookup page is your product database browser. It's similar to the Sell page but focused on product information rather than sales.
>
> Use the search bar to find products by name, SKU, or brand. The advanced filters let you narrow results by brand, stock level, price range, and sort order.
>
> Click any product to see its full details in the right panel. You'll see the product image, price, stock level, location in your store, and description.
>
> From here, you can add the product to a sale, edit its details, or delete it. The 'Add' button at the top lets you create new products.
>
> This is also where you'd manage your product catalog - updating prices, descriptions, and stock information."

### Actions
1. Navigate to Lookup page
2. Search for a product
3. Click "Filters" to show advanced options
4. Select a category
5. Click a product to view details
6. Scroll through the detail panel
7. Point to Edit and Delete buttons
8. Click "Add" to show create modal (then cancel)

### Expected Results
- Products list displays
- Filters work correctly
- Detail panel shows product info
- Create modal opens

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Filter panel expand animation
- [ ] Product selection highlight
- [ ] Detail panel scroll indicator
- [ ] Action buttons callout

## Chapter 6: Inventory Management (11:00 - 13:00)

### Narration
> "The Inventory page gives you complete control over your stock. At the top, you'll see key metrics: total items, low stock count, out of stock items, and pending receiving.
>
> The page has five tabs. 'Inventory' shows your current stock list. 'Receiving' is for processing incoming shipments. 'Transfers' handles stock movement between locations. 'Vendor Bills' connects to the document processing system. 'Alerts' shows items needing attention.
>
> In the main inventory list, you can search, select multiple items for bulk actions, and see stock levels at a glance. The status badges show green for in-stock, yellow for low stock, and red for out of stock.
>
> The 'Scan' button opens a barcode scanner for quick product lookup. You can use your device's camera or enter barcodes manually.
>
> Select multiple items to access bulk actions: Print Labels, Adjust Stock, or Transfer to another location."

### Actions
1. Navigate to Inventory page
2. Point to statistics cards
3. Click through each tab
4. Search for a product
5. Click "Scan" to show scanner modal
6. Select multiple items using checkboxes
7. Show bulk action buttons
8. Click "Alerts" tab to show low stock items

### Expected Results
- Inventory list loads
- Tabs switch correctly
- Scanner modal opens
- Bulk selection works

### Visual Cues
- [ ] Stats cards highlight
- [ ] Tab switching animation
- [ ] Scanner modal demo
- [ ] Bulk selection indicator
- [ ] Alert badge on tab

## Chapter 8: Admin & Settings (14:30 - 16:30)

### Narration
> "The Admin section is where you configure your entire system. On desktop, you'll see a sidebar with all settings categories. On mobile, these appear as scrollable tabs.
>
> Let's look at the key sections:
>
> 'Users & Roles' lets you create user accounts, assign roles like Admin, Manager, or Cashier, and manage permissions.
>
> 'Company & Stores' is where you set up your business information and store locations.
>
> 'Tax Rules' configures your tax rates and rules.
>
> 'Hardware' connects printers, scanners, and payment terminals.
>
> 'Data Management' handles backups, imports, and exports.
>
> 'Integrations' connects to external services like QuickBooks for accounting.
>
> 'Feature Flags' lets you enable or disable specific features.
>
> Take time to explore each section and configure the system for your business needs."

## Chapter 9: Documents & OCR (16:30 - 17:30)

### Narration
> "The Documents section handles vendor invoice processing with OCR - Optical Character Recognition.
>
> Upload a PDF invoice, and the system automatically extracts product information, quantities, and prices. Review the extracted data, make corrections if needed, and approve to import into your inventory.
>
> The Statistics cards show documents needing review, currently processing, and any that failed.
>
> The 'Next Action Center' suggests what you should do next based on your queue status.
>
> Use the Processing Queue tab to monitor active OCR jobs.
>
> This feature dramatically speeds up inventory receiving from vendor shipments."

### Actions
1. Navigate to Documents page
2. Point to statistics cards
3. Show Next Action Center
4. Click Processing Queue tab
5. Point to Upload button
6. (If documents exist) Click a document to show detail

### Expected Results
- Documents page loads
- Statistics display
- Tabs switch correctly

## Chapter 10: Conclusion & Tips (17:30 - 18:30)

### Narration
> "That covers the main features of EasySale. Here are some tips for getting the most out of the system:
>
> First, set up your products and categories before going live. Use the import feature for bulk data entry.
>
> Second, train your staff on the Sell page - it's designed for speed and efficiency.
>
> Third, regularly check the Inventory alerts to prevent stockouts.
>
> Fourth, use customer profiles to build loyalty and track purchasing patterns.
>
> Fifth, remember that EasySale works offline. If you lose internet, keep working - your data will sync when connectivity returns.
>
> For more help, check the documentation or contact support. Thank you for choosing EasySale!"

## Production Notes

### Recording Requirements
- Clean browser with no extensions visible
- Demo data populated (products, customers, transactions)
- Stable internet connection for sync status
- 1920x1080 resolution
- Dark theme (default)

### Audio Requirements
- Professional voiceover
- Background music (subtle, non-distracting)
- Sound effects for clicks/transitions (optional)

### Post-Production
- Add chapter markers
- Include captions/subtitles
- Add callout animations
- Compress for web delivery

1. Full guide (18 minutes)
